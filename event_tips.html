Celebrate with Sabering

Beginning with Napoleon in France as an interesting way to expedite opening a bottle of Champagne, the art of sabrage has grown into an integral part of celebrations and special occasions around the world.

Kevin Howard, a certified member of the exclusive “Club des Sabruers” is one of an elite group of professional sabreurs who can add a special touch to a wedding toast, your next fund-raising event, or other milestone celebrations. Kevin has opened every sized bottle – from 750ml to 20l – and works with all of his clients to customize an experience that will enhance any type of gala. The sabering involves actually cutting through the bottle’s neck, leaving the cork completely intact – which then becomes a wonderful event memento.

Sabering can be integrated into any toast or program. Additionally, Kevin is happy to share an interesting 5-10 minute talk about the history of sabering, from its beginnings in France to a recap of its legacy today. This legacy includes sabering events worldwide, as the art has continued in the hands of individuals like Julia Child and Kevin’s own friend and mentor Robert Gourdin.
